Meta Pricing

Meta pricing is fundamentally based on conversations and template categories. Understanding this model is essential for managing your business costs effectively on the WAPI platform.

1

1. Overview

  • Meta pricing is fundamentally based on:
    • Conversations (measured in 24-hour windows).
    • Template messages (the new category-based pricing model).
  • Difference in Models:
    • Conversation-based pricing (older model): Charged one flat rate for a 24-hour session regardless of content.
    • Per-template message pricing (current model): Charges are determined by the specific category of the template message sent to initiate or continue the interaction.
2

2. What is a Conversation

  • A conversation equals a 24-hour continuous messaging window.
  • When a conversation starts:
    • A user sends a message to the business (user-initiated).
    • A business delivers an approved template to a user (business-initiated).
  • What happens inside the 24-hour window:
    • Free-form messages (text, media, non-templates) are allowed.
    • There is no additional conversation charge for exchanging these free-form messages.
3

3. Customer Service Window (24 Hours)

  • When it starts: The moment a user's message is delivered to the business.
  • What actions are allowed:
    • Sending free-form messages to assist the user.
    • Sending Utility templates (which in some continuous conversational scenarios may not incur additional charges depending on the existing open category).
  • What happens after it expires:
    • The business can only send pre-approved template messages to re-engage the user.
    • Sending these templates will open a new window and charges will apply based on the template category.
4

4. Conversation Types / Categories

Meta categorizes all templates into distinct types, dictating the cost.

1. Marketing

Promotional offers, sales, newsletters. Highest cost category.

2. Utility

Transactional updates, order alerts, reminders. Low cost.

3. Authentication

OTP and verification codes. Medium cost.

4. Service

Customer support inquiries. First 1,000/mo are free.

5

5. Free Messages / Free Scenarios

  • Messages inside the 24-hour window: Any standard free-form text or media messages sent in response to a user are free.
  • Service conversations: The first 1,000 user-initiated service conversations per month per WhatsApp Business Account (WABA) are free.
  • Utility Contexts: Under some specific conditions, utility messages sent inside open service/marketing windows are free.
6

6. Key Pricing Rules Summary

  • No payment per API call: You are not charged for the HTTP requests themselves.
  • Charges depend entirely on:
    • Template usage (what type of template was delivered).
    • Conversation timing (whether the 24-hour window is active).
    • Category of the message (Marketing, Utility, Authentication, Service).
7

7. Best Practices

  • Minimize template usage: Only send templates when absolutely necessary to initiate a flow.
  • Stay within 24-hour window: Resolve customer problems and push updates inside the active service window using free-form messages.
  • Use Utility instead of Marketing when possible: If a message is purely transactional (e.g., shipping update), ensure it uses a Utility template to benefit from lower rates.
  • Optimize conversation flow: Combine messages where logical rather than stringing multiple disjointed templates, which stacks charges.

Pricing Details

Marketing: $0.02 – $0.15
Utility: $0.003 – $0.03
Authentication: $0.005 – $0.05
Service: Free

*Note: Pricing varies by country. India pricing is lower than US/EU.

Important Clarifications:

  • WhatsApp Flows are NOT charged separately.
  • Webhooks are always free.
  • API calls are free.
  • Charges are triggered only by template usage.

Example Scenarios:

  • User sends message → reply → FREE
  • Send marketing template → CHARGED
  • Send flow inside 24h → FREE
  • Send flow via template → CHARGED
